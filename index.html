Critical Defense Strategies:

1. Security Awareness Training

Regular phishing simulations and training would have helped Muskan identify the fake email. Employees are the first line of defense.

2. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even if Muskan clicked the link, MFA would have prevented attackers from accessing systems with stolen credentials.

3. Regular Offline Backups

Air-gapped backups (physically disconnected from network) allow recovery without paying ransom. Test backups regularly!

4. Network Segmentation

Isolating critical systems limits ransomware spread. If Muskan's PC was segmented, servers wouldn't be affected.

5. Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR)

EDR tools detect and block ransomware behavior in real-time, stopping encryption before it starts.

6. Email Security Filters

Advanced filters detect spoofed domains and malicious links, blocking phishing emails before they reach inboxes.

7. Incident Response Plan

Pre-planned response procedures minimize damage and recovery time when attacks occur.
